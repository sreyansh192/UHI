Urban Heat effect

What is Urban Heat Island effect??

The temperature of urban areas are significantly higher than that of the surrounding rural areas. This fact is known as the "Urban Heat Island" effect.

Factors work to promote a heat island.

Concrete and man made surfaces Less water in the soil and environment Excess energy from buildings Excess waste generation Lack of Evapotranspiration locking of wind by tall Buildings Results of above points increase concentration of Ozone which results in temp. increment

Causes

The main cause of the UHI effect modification of land surfaces, and materials that effectively store short- wave radiation. Blocking of winds by tall buildings. Waste heat generated by energy usage is a secondary contributor factories. During the day in rural areas evaporation and evapotranspiration causes cooling. In cities,large solar energy are absorbed by the concrete structures. UHIs have been indirectly related to all climatic change and greenhouse effect. Night are warmer in urban because of radiation from block surfaces in atmosphere. Tar, asphalt, brick and concrete are main absorber and radiator unlike soil.
